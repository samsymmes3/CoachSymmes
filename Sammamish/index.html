  Kingco Tracker displays relavant information on the current standings for qualifying for the Kingco league meet (May 12 & 13), broken down by athlete and by event, as well as the outlook on the team competition at Kingco.

  There are 16 spots in each event for athletes at the Kingco League meet. These spots will be given to the 16 athletes with the best marks this season with a couple exceptions. For vertical jumps, you must be able to hit the official opening height in addition to being top 16. If you are not quite top 16, you may still get a spot if another athlete scratches an event. Scatches are very common (a few may drop) in the 400m, and 800m because they are hard to double with other events, and jumping events because the sprinters, who are often also the jumpers, fill up their four event limit fairly quickly.

By Event: As of right now, who's in Kingco (if you're close to number 16, people are going to be gunning for your spot), who's close, and what the number 16 mark is.

By Individual: As of right now, what events is each person in, or close to in Kingco.

By Team: How does our team stack up? Scores on the left, what events each team is scoring in on the right.

  State Tracker displays the current outlook for qualifying for the State meet (May 25-27), as well as the team's outlook at State.

  Unlike Kingco, you do not qualify for the State meet with times/marks posted during the season. This means the times listed as a "cutoff" are estimates based on marks people have posted so far this year. To qualify to for State, you must qualify through rounds in the post season. If you finish top five at Kingco, you qualify for the Bi-District meet the following week against District 3. If you finish top six at the Bi-District meet, you earn a spot at State. This format means that athletes have to be able to perform in a single race, so it's a great opportunity to earn a spot even if other people have posted better marks than you this season. Last year, our athletes performed very well at Districts, and we got to take a lot of kids to State, so hopefully this year will be the same!

By Event: As of right now, who's marks would get them to State (assuming you make Districts), who's close, and what is the sixth best mark headed into the Districts meet.

By Individual: Listing each person who looks like they have a shot to make a run at state, and what their rank is between districts 2 and 3.

By Team: How does our team stack up against the whole state?
