WIAA 4A XC Rankings

How were these made?

  The team rankings were built by using athlete data over the past 13 years, and each returning athlete's cross country and track performances to generate a probability distribution of times for the upcoming season. 10,000 simulations were run giving each athlete a random time within their distribution, removing a small portion of athletes as injured, and randomly distributing freshman to teams based on their team size. The teams were then ranked by their ability to score in a 16 team race of similar teams. The number next to a team name is the average place in these simulations, and the time next to each athlete is the 50th percentile in their projected time distribution, and was provided so you can get an idea of how a team at each rank is built.

  The individual rankings were built as a combination of rankings at the end of last season, State finishes, and track results and times. The "Reference Time" is representative of a very good race on an average course from the 2023 season. To build this time, courses were abstracted out by weighting based on average times, then large meets and meets where a particular athlete ran well were weighted more heavily to build a time that could be expected on an average day.

  I have only ranked 25 teams and individuals, but there are 60 teams in 4A this year, 1000 returning girls, and 2000 returning boys. Making the rankings means a team is above average, and an individual is competing at an elite level.
